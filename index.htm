Open Educational Thinkering

Hey, thanks for stopping by. I call myself an 'Open Educational Thinkerer' which I use as a kind of shorthand for working at the intersection of education, technology, and productivity.

Right now I'm a consultant and spend my time helping people like you and organisations such as yours think through digital literacies, Open Badges, and to improve internal communication and workflows.

I'm based in the North-East of England, working on a global scale and travelling extensively. I began doing so when I joined the Mozilla Foundation in 2012, becoming first as their Badges & Skills Lead, and then Web Literacy Lead. I led work with the worldwide community on versions of the Web Literacy Map, which underpins all of the work of the Foundation.

Mozilla asked me to work for them while I was working in Higher Education for Jisc on mobile learning, Open Educational Resources, and digital literacies. Prior to that I was a teacher and senior leader in schools, most recently Director of E-Learning at (what at the time was) the largest academy in England.

I'm known for my analytical mind (my first degree was in Philosophy), infectious enthusiasm, collaborative spirit, open ways of working, and my evangelism of missions, tools, and processes that I believe in. My guiding principle in life, which I've extended to my work through Dynamic Skillset is to work with awesome people who are doing things to make the world a better place.
